**7. Servings:** 4

**12. Equipment:** Steamer basket or a pan with a lid and rack, pot, immersion blender or food processor

**13. Ingredients (for 4 servings):**
- Skinless salmon fillet — 4 pieces (~150 g / 5.3 oz each)
- Fresh dill — 3 tablespoons, chopped (plus extra for garnish)
- Plain unsweetened yogurt (Greek or regular) — 120 g (about 1/2 cup)
- Lemon juice — 1 tablespoon
- Lemon zest — 1/2 teaspoon
- Garlic — 1 small clove, grated
- Cauliflower — 1 medium head (~600 g / 1.3 lb), cut into florets
- Low-sodium vegetable broth — 100 ml (about 1/3 cup + 1 tbsp)
- Extra virgin olive oil — 1 tablespoon (15 ml)
- Green beans — 200 g (7 oz), optional, for serving
- Salt — to taste (about 1/4 teaspoon)
- Black pepper — to taste

**14. Method:**
1. Boil or steam the cauliflower for 12–15 minutes, until very tender.
2. Meanwhile, make the sauce: combine the yogurt, chopped dill, lemon juice and zest, grated garlic, salt and pepper. Refrigerate until ready to use.
3. Steam the salmon for 8–10 minutes (depending on thickness), until it reaches a safe internal temperature of 63 °C (145 °F) and flakes easily with a fork. If desired, steam the green beans alongside the salmon for the last 5 minutes.
4. Drain the cauliflower well and blend it with the vegetable broth and olive oil until smooth. Adjust salt to taste.
5. Serve the salmon over a bed of cauliflower purée, topped with the dill sauce, with the green beans on the side.

**15. Tips:** If the purée turns out too thin, cook it 2–3 minutes longer over low heat, stirring, to let the excess liquid evaporate.

**16. Common mistakes to avoid:** Don't pan-fry the salmon in a lot of oil or cover it in a thick cream- or butter-based sauce — that turns it into a heavy dinner, which is exactly what this dish is designed to avoid. Also avoid overcooking the salmon: past its point it dries out and loses juiciness.

**17. Possible substitutions:** Salmon can be swapped for trout or a firm white fish (hake, grouper); cauliflower for broccoli in the purée; Greek yogurt for regular plain yogurt.

**18. Allergy alternatives:**
- Dairy-free: replace the yogurt in the sauce with unsweetened coconut yogurt.
- Fish-free: this recipe has no direct fish-free swap — for a fish allergy, see other dinners in the collection featuring plant protein or chicken.

**19. Storage:** Refrigerate the salmon, sauce, and purée in separate airtight containers.

**20. Refrigeration/freezing time:** Up to 2 days refrigerated (cooked fish doesn't keep as long as other proteins). Freezing after cooking is not recommended, as it affects texture.

**21. Reheating instructions:** Reheat the salmon gently, covered, over low heat or in the microwave at medium power for 1 minute, being careful not to dry it out. The dill sauce is served cold and should not be reheated.

**22. Serving suggestion:** A chamomile tea after dinner, or a small portion of brown rice if a bit more staying power is needed.

**23. Budget version:** Replace the salmon with seasonal white fish, which is generally more affordable, and use regular plain yogurt instead of Greek yogurt.

**24. Vegetarian/vegan version:** Replace the salmon with firm tofu, pan-seared with a pinch of salt and lemon, keeping the cauliflower purée and dill sauce (use coconut yogurt for a fully vegan version).
